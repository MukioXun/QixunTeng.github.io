About Me (滕奇勋)

Hi, I am an undergraduate student at Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT), specializing in Spoken Large Language Models (Spoken LLMs) and Paralinguistics. My current research focuses on modeling and evaluating nuanced vocal attributes—such as emotion, tone, and pacing—to build more expressive and human-like speech agents.

My technical journey is defined by cross-stack exploration. I have led the development of a monolithic OS kernel in Rust (National 2nd Prize), published research in Computer Vision (JCR Q1), and designed RAG frameworks for embodied AI. This unique blend of low-level systems engineering and high-level deep learning allows me to approach AI challenges with both architectural rigor and algorithmic innovation.

Currently, I am working on establishing standardized benchmarks for speech capabilities, aiming to fill the gap in multi-dimensional semantic and paralinguistic understanding within the open-source community.
